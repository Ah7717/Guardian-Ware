Welcome to Guardian Wear

Empowering Independence, Ensuring Safety

Understanding the Challenge

Children under five with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and related developmental conditions often struggle with loud noises, unfamiliar environments, or sudden changes in routine. These can lead to sensory overload, meltdowns, or complete withdrawal, making daily life overwhelming.

For caregivers, managing these reactions requires constant vigilance, which can be both mentally and physically draining. Striking a balance between keeping a child safe and encouraging independence is difficult.

Current Limitations

While technologies like nanny cams or smart sensors offer help, they restrict children to confined, controlled environments. True freedom requires tools that support them wherever they go, not just in pre-defined “safe zones.”

Early intervention and structured support are key to helping children with mental disorders build confidence and life skills. Adaptive, mobile technology can make this more achievable.

Empowering Independence

The ultimate goal is to help children navigate the world safely and independently, while easing the stress on caregivers. By combining personalized support with compassionate tech, we can open up more possibilities for growth and emotional well-being for both children and their families.

Proposed Solution: Smart Wearables

Children with mental disabilities frequently face panic attacks, anxiety episodes, and emotional meltdowns that hinder independent functioning. These wearables would offer real-time support by monitoring physiological signals and detecting signs of distress early.

Using biosensors, the devices can trigger calming feedback like vibrations, breathing guides, or caregiver alerts—helping children regain control and continue with their activities.

Overview

This safety system is designed for young children aged 3 to 5, aiming to balance independence and safety. It includes three main components: a pair of smart glasses, a specialized smartwatch, and a parent-facing companion app. These elements work together to monitor the child's environment and health, and provide instant communication when needed.

Smart Glasses

The glasses are lightweight and comfortable, ensuring the child can wear them without discomfort. They feature a micro-lens that captures the child's point of view and transmits it to the smartwatch using Bluetooth. The glasses include a sealed, removable battery that is charged externally, making them resistant to water, mud, and dust, and removing the need for ports or onboard storage.

Smartwatch

Designed to look like a regular smartwatch, it’s both affordable and discreet. It houses biometric sensors to track heart rate and blood oxygen levels, and connects to the glasses to buffer video data before transmitting it to the app. With a SIM card slot, it supports internet connectivity and calling. The side dial acts as an SOS button, playing a pre-recorded parent message and initiating emergency calls to guardians. It runs on a rechargeable, removable battery.

Companion App

The app, used by parents, receives updates every 100ms from the watch. It displays real-time video and biometric data, alerts parents when thresholds are crossed, and detects visual triggers using a zero-shot object detection AI model. Parents can view the child’s environment, receive and make calls, and configure SOS responses through the app interface.

Benefits

This system helps young children explore safely and gives parents peace of mind. It combines real-time health and visual monitoring with smart alerts and communication tools, enabling quick responses to any situation while supporting a child’s natural independence.
